SynthNet

Decentralized synthetic data generation on Bittensor

Subnet idea & current focus

In one sentence

SynthNet is a Bittensor subnet that acts as a decentralized marketplace for synthetic data: users request datasets (text, code, or conversations), miners produce them, and validators score quality on-chain so that supply, demand, and rewards align without a central operator.

What we've worked on

  • Miner role design — miners generate datasets; trust, incentive, and dividends metrics.
  • Validator role design — validators have permits and score miner output.
  • Task assignment / scoring logic and evaluation mechanisms (in design and UI).

Main challenges

  • Designing sustainable incentives (biggest focus)
  • Defining a clear proof of intelligence or effort
  • Evaluation and scoring design
  • Preventing adversarial or low-quality behavior

Biggest blocker

Defining incentive curves and emission rules so that miners are rewarded for high-quality synthetic data rather than volume, and validators for accurate scoring, without making emissions unsustainable or easy to game. Still figuring out how to tie TAO flow to measurable quality (e.g. downstream usefulness or human/validator agreement) in a way that stays simple and on-chain.
